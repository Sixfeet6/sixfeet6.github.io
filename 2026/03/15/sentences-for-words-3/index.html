Sentences for Words-3

To help you understand the words from your March 11 list, I’ve created one sentence for each. Happy studying! :)

  1. strive: We must strive to maintain a balance between our study sessions and rest.
  2. depict: The textbook uses several diagrams to depict the behavior of light in different media.
  3. neglect: It is easy to neglect the importance of review when we are busy with new physics problems.
  4. sophisticated: Modern labs use sophisticated equipment to measure the precise speed of light.
  5. exert: When you push a block, you exert a force that causes it to accelerate.
  6. corporation: After graduation, some physics students choose to work for a high-tech corporation.
  7. grant: The university decided to grant a scholarship to the student who excelled in Thermodynamics.
  8. adolescent: During their adolescent years, many people develop a deep interest in philosophy.
  9. adverse: High temperatures can have an adverse effect on the performance of electronic components.
  10. strike: The workers decided to go on strike to demand better safety conditions in the factory.
  11. reverse: If you reverse the direction of the current, the magnetic field will also change its orientation.
  12. mutual: Successful group study depends on mutual respect and support among team members.
  13. curriculum: The physics curriculum at LZU is designed to build a strong foundation in classical mechanics first.
  14. remedy: A clear set of notes is often the best remedy for confusion before an exam.
  15. accumulate: Small daily reviews will help you accumulate a vast amount of knowledge over the semester.
  16. render: A sudden power outage can render the laboratory computers useless during an experiment.
  17. counterpart: The student discussed his research with his counterpart at another university.
  18. foster: Engaging in deep discussions can foster a better understanding of complex scientific theories.
  19. fierce: There is fierce competition among students who want to enter the Cuiying Academy.
  20. entrepreneur: A successful entrepreneur needs to be both innovative and willing to take risks.
  21. chronic: Lack of sleep can lead to chronic fatigue, which hinders your ability to solve physics problems.
  22. cater: Some online courses cater specifically to students who prefer visual learning.
  23. deem: Teachers deem it necessary to master basic calculus before moving on to electromagnetism.
  24. contemporary: We studied both classical theories and contemporary developments in quantum physics.
  25. refrain: Please refrain from using your phone during the three-hour physics lecture.
  26. portray: Scientific biographies often portray Isaac Newton as a solitary and brilliant figure.
  27. skeptical: Scientists are naturally skeptical and require experimental proof for every new theory.
  28. allocate: You should allocate at least two hours a day for solving optics problems.
  29. whereas: Mechanics deals with macroscopic objects, whereas quantum physics focuses on the subatomic level.
  30. hinder: Noise in the library can hinder your concentration during a difficult study session.
  31. boom: The recent boom in technology has created many new jobs for physics graduates.
  32. collective: Solving the problem required the collective effort of the entire research team.
  33. implement: The professor plans to implement a new grading system next semester.
  34. leap: The discovery of the electron represented a giant leap forward in our understanding of matter.
  35. deteriorate: Without proper maintenance, the precision of the experimental instruments will deteriorate.
  36. postpone: We had to postpone our library session because pyym had a sudden meeting.
  37. recession: During an economic recession, many companies reduce their research and development budgets.
  38. welfare: The government should invest more in the welfare of university students and researchers.
  39. unprecedented: The recent breakthroughs in fusion energy are truly unprecedented in history.
  40. infrastructure: A good laboratory infrastructure is essential for conducting high-level physics research.
  41. radical: Einstein’s theory of relativity introduced a radical change in how we perceive time and space.
  42. tremendous: It takes a tremendous amount of energy to accelerate particles to near the speed of light.
  43. slip: Be careful not to let your hand slip while you are adjusting the delicate optical lenses.
  44. irrational: It is irrational to expect to master thermodynamics in a single afternoon.
  45. commission: The university set up a commission to review the new physics safety protocols.
  46. sustain: We need a steady supply of power to sustain the magnetic field in the experiment.
  47. recruit: High-tech companies often visit LZU to recruit promising physics students.
  48. alleviate: Taking a short walk can help alleviate the stress of preparing for finals.
  49. deprive: Do not deprive yourself of sleep, as it is crucial for memory consolidation.
  50. migrate: Some birds migrate thousands of miles to find warmer climates in the winter.
  51. stem: Many problems in advanced physics stem from a weak understanding of basic calculus.
  52. reluctant: Sitson was reluctant to leave the library until he finished the last optics problem.
  53. exploit: We should learn how to exploit the properties of lasers for more accurate measurements.
  54. compel: The strict rules of the lab compel every researcher to wear protective gear.
  55. imperative: It is imperative to double-check your calculations before publishing your results.
  56. exotic: The physicist presented a theory about exotic matter that exists only in the early universe.
  57. scheme: The city proposed a new scheme to improve public transportation for students.
  58. resort: After trying many methods, his last resort was to ask the professor for a hint.
  59. flourish: With proper funding, the new research center is expected to flourish.
  60. persist: If the experimental error should persist, we must recalibrate the sensors.
  61. resolve: We need to resolve the conflict between the two sets of experimental data.
  62. correlate: Scientists found that the two variables correlate strongly in high-pressure conditions.
  63. magnify: You can use a convex lens to magnify the image of a small object.
  64. deplete: Running the high-power laser for too long will quickly deplete the battery.
  65. entail: Becoming a great physicist will entail many years of hard work and dedication.
  66. scratch: Sometimes you have to start from scratch if your initial assumptions are wrong.
  67. secondary: In this experiment, the temperature change is a secondary effect that we can ignore.
