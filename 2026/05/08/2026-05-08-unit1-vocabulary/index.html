Unit 1 单词集锦：苏格拉底与东方圣人

🎬 Unit 1 单词集锦 (Dialogue Edition)

这些单词选自 Unit 1 的 iExplore 1 & 2，主题涵盖了古希腊哲人苏格拉底以及东方的孔子及其思想。为了方便记忆，我把它们处理成了带有“台词感”的句子。:)

Part 1: The Athenian Gadfly (iExplore 1)

  1. philosopher [fəˈlɑːsəfər]

    • 释义: 哲学家 (A seeker of wisdom and truth)
    • 台词: “He calls himself a philosopher, but most days he just stands in the market asking people why they exist until they cry.”
    • 翻译: “他自称是哲学家，但大多数时候他只是站在集市里问人们为什么要活着，直到把人家问哭。”

  2. patron saint [ˈpeɪtrən seɪnt]

    • 释义: 守护神，保护圣徒 (A saint who protects a particular place or group)
    • 台词: “In this laboratory, coffee is our patron saint. Without it, the laws of physics cease to apply.”
    • 翻译: “在这个实验室里，咖啡就是我们的守护神。没有它，物理定律就不再适用了。”

  3. snub-nosed [ˈsnʌbˌnoʊzd]

    • 释义: 扁平鼻子的 (Having a short, flat nose)
    • 台词: “Don’t be fooled by his snub-nosed, friendly face; he can dismantle your argument in three seconds flat.”
    • 翻译: “别被他那塌鼻子、和蔼可亲的脸给骗了；他能在三秒钟内拆解你的论点。”

  4. podgy [ˈpɑːdʒi]

    • 释义: 矮胖的，胖乎乎的 (Chubby or slightly fat)
    • 台词: “The podgy little man surprisingly outran the city guards. Never underestimate the power of sheer panic.”
    • 翻译: “那个矮胖的小个子竟然跑赢了城卫兵。永远不要低估惊慌失措爆发出的力量。”

  5. gadfly [ˈɡædflaɪ]

    • 释义: 牛虻；惹人讨厌但能起催促作用的人 (An annoying person who provokes others into action)
    • 台词: “I’m not trying to be difficult. Every society needs a gadfly to keep it from falling into a comfortable sleep.”
    • 翻译: “我不是在故意刁难。每个社会都需要一只‘牛虻’，好让它不至于陷入安逸的沉睡。”

  6. razor-sharp [ˈreɪzər ʃɑːrp]

    • 释义: 极锋利的；极敏锐的 (Extremely sharp or keen)
    • 台词: “His logic is razor-sharp. If you’re going to lie to him, you’d better be prepared to bleed.”
    • 翻译: “他的逻辑极度敏锐。如果你打算对他撒谎，最好做好流血的准备。”

  7. deceitful [dɪˈsiːtfʊl]

    • 释义: 欺骗的，不诚实的 (Dishonest; misleading)
    • 台词: “A deceitful peace is often more dangerous than an honest war.”
    • 翻译: “虚伪的和平往往比诚实的战争更危险。”

  8. disconcerting [ˌdɪskənˈsɜːrtɪŋ]

    • 释义: 令人不安的，窘迫的 (Causing one to feel unsettled or confused)
    • 台词: “The way she stares at the chalkboard without blinking is deeply disconcerting.”
    • 翻译: “她盯着黑板看却不眨眼的样子，真是让人感到莫名的不安。”

  9. oracle [ˈɔːrəkl]

    • 释义: 神谕；传神谕者 (A priestess acting as a medium for divine advice)
    • 台词: “The oracle told me I would be the wisest man in Athens, mostly because I’m the only one who admits he knows nothing.”
    • 翻译: “神谕告诉我，我会是雅典最聪明的人，主要是因为我是唯一一个承认自己一无所知的人。”

  10. undermine [ˌʌndərˈmaɪn]

    • 释义: 暗中破坏，削弱 (To weaken or damage gradually)
    • 台词: “If you keep questioning my orders in front of the crew, you’ll undermine my authority entirely.”
    • 翻译: “如果你继续在船员面前质疑我的命令，你就会彻底削弱我的威信。”

Part 2: The Sage and the Doctrine (iExplore 2)

  1. sage [seɪdʒ]

    • 释义: 圣人，贤哲 (A profoundly wise person)
    • 台词: “The old sage lived on top of the mountain, not for the view, but to avoid the people who asked for stock market tips.”
    • 翻译: “那位老圣人住在山顶，不是为了看风景，而是为了避开那些找他问股市建议的人。”

  2. reactionary [riˈækʃəneri]

    • 释义: 反动者，保守分子 (Opposing political or social progress)
    • 台词: “He’s such a reactionary that he thinks the invention of the wheel was a step too far.”
    • 翻译: “他是个极其保守的人，甚至觉得轮子的发明都有些过头了。”

  3. bolster [ˈboʊlstər]

    • 释义: 支持，巩固 (To support or strengthen)
    • 台词: “We need more data to bolster our hypothesis, or the committee will laugh us out of the room.”
    • 翻译: “我们需要更多数据来支撑我们的假设，否则委员会会把我们哄出去的。”

  4. aristocracy [ˌærɪˈstɑːkrəsi]

    • 释义: 贵族，统治阶层 (The highest class in certain societies)
    • 台词: “The aristocracy treats the rest of us like background characters in their own personal play.”
    • 翻译: “那些贵族把我们其余的人都当成了他们个人剧作里的背景龙套。”

  5. menial [ˈmiːniəl]

    • 释义: 卑微的，不需要技能的 (Lowly and boring work)
    • 台词: “I started by doing menial tasks like cleaning the floor. Now, I’m the one who decides who cleans the floor.”
    • 翻译: “我从扫地这种卑微的杂活做起。现在，由我来决定谁负责扫地。”

  6. swashbuckling [ˈswɒʃˌbʌklɪŋ]

    • 释义: 敢做敢为的，神勇的 (Daring and flamboyant adventure)
    • 台词: “I miss the swashbuckling days of my youth, before my knees decided they didn’t like stairs anymore.”
    • 翻译: “我怀念年轻时那些神勇冒险的日子，在那之后，我的膝盖就开始抗议走楼梯了。”

  7. intrigue [ɪnˈtriːɡ]

    • 释义: 阴谋，诡计 (Secret planning of something illegal or harmful)
    • 台词: “The court is a nest of intrigue. Don’t eat anything you haven’t seen someone else taste first.”
    • 翻译: “朝廷就是个阴谋之巢。别吃任何你没见过别人先尝过的东西。”

  8. ruthlessly [ˈruːθləsli]

    • 释义: 无情地，冷酷地 (Without pity or compassion)
    • 台词: “To succeed in this business, you have to be ruthlessly efficient. Mistakes are for the bankrupt.”
    • 翻译: “在这个行业要想成功，你必须冷酷地追求效率。错误是留给破产者的。”

  9. eccentric [ɪkˈsentrɪk]

    • 释义: 古怪的，异乎寻常的 (Unconventional and slightly strange)
    • 台词: “He’s not ‘crazy’; he’s just wealthy enough to be called eccentric.”
    • 翻译: “他不是‘疯了’；他只是足够富有，所以才被称作‘古怪’。”

  10. disciple [dɪˈsaɪpl]

    • 释义: 信徒，追随者 (A follower or student of a teacher or leader)
    • 台词: “I was his most loyal disciple, until I realized his ‘philosophy’ was just an excuse to avoid paying taxes.”
    • 翻译: “我曾是他最忠诚的信徒，直到我意识到他的‘哲学’只是逃税的借口。”

Phrases & Expressions (短语篇)

  1. take off

    • 释义: 急速离开；脱掉；（事业）腾飞
    • 台词: “If the guards see us, we need to take off immediately. Don’t look back for your hat.”
    • 翻译: “要是卫兵看见我们，我们得立刻开溜。别回头捡你的帽子了。”

  2. be tied in knots

    • 释义: 极其不安；被难住
    • 台词: “Every time I have to give a speech, my stomach is tied in knots.”
    • 翻译: “每次我得演说时，我的胃都紧张得拧成了团。”

  3. border on

    • 释义: 接近，近乎 (To be very close to something)
    • 台词: “His confidence borders on arrogance, but with results like those, who can blame him?”
    • 翻译: “他的自信近乎狂妄，但有了那样的战果，谁又能责怪他呢？”

  4. get the better of

    • 释义: 战胜，占上风 (To defeat or outsmart)
    • 台词: “Don’t let your curiosity get the better of you. Some doors are locked for a reason.”
    • 翻译: “别让你的好奇心占了上风。有些门被锁上是有原因的。”

  5. set off

    • 释义: 出发；引发
    • 台词: “We set off at dawn. If you’re not at the gate, we’re leaving without you.”
    • 翻译: “我们黎明出发。如果你不在门口，我们就丢下你自己走了。”
